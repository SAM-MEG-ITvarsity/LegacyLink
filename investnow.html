2. Regulatory Framework and Compliance

2.1 Financial Sector Regulation Act 9 of 2017 (FSRA): This Act establishes the regulatory structure for financial services in South Africa — including the creation of the Financial Sector Conduct Authority (FSCA) and the Prudential Authority (PA) under the South African Reserve Bank (SARB). It provides powers to regulate financial product providers, ensure market conduct, and protect financial customers. LegacyLink complies with these obligations by maintaining transparent operations and consumer protection standards.

2.2 Financial Advisory and Intermediary Services Act 37 of 2002 (FAIS): Governs the licensing and conduct of providers who give advice or intermediary services relating to financial products. It ensures consumers are provided with adequate information and that those who advise are competent and properly authorised.

2.3 Protection of Investment Act 22 of 2015: Provides legislative protection for investors and their investments in South Africa — ensuring a balance between rights and obligations of investors and the state. LegacyLink upholds these principles by maintaining transparent business operations, honouring investor rights, and providing access to fair dispute resolution mechanisms if necessary.

2.4 Financial Markets Act 19 of 2012: Ensures the fairness, efficiency, and transparency of financial markets in South Africa. LegacyLink is committed to maintaining market integrity and providing accurate, up-to-date information regarding investment performance, market conditions, and risk disclosures.
